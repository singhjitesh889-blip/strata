NEW ENTRANTS — D2C JUICE SPACE (Watch List)
Updated: June 2026

--- Raw Mango Co (Bangalore) ---
Founded: 2024
Funding: Rs 2Cr seed (Dec 2024)
Focus: Premium single-origin mango products. Only one SKU — Alphonso mango juice. Very premium, very limited.
Distribution: D2C only. Website + Instagram.
Marketing: Micro-influencer. Food photographers. Niche.
Threat level: LOW for now. Too niche, too small, too seasonal.
Watch for: If they expand to other fruits with same quality positioning.

--- Jungle Fruit (Delhi) ---
Founded: 2023
Funding: Rs 5Cr Series A (March 2026) — ACTIVE GROWTH PHASE
Focus: "Jungle-sourced" exotic fruits. Dragon fruit, passion fruit, star fruit juices. Urban novelty positioning.
Distribution: D2C + Zepto partnership announced. Quick commerce growing.
Marketing: Heavy on Instagram content. Influencer-forward. Aesthetic-first.
Threat level: MEDIUM. Well-funded, quick commerce distribution, younger audience. Not our core SKU overlap but competing for same wallet.
Watch for: If they enter Mango/Kokum/traditional flavor territory.

--- Nimbooz Craft (Ahmedabad) ---
Founded: 2025
Funding: Unknown (bootstrapped likely)
Focus: Craft lemonade variants. Regional, Gujarati consumer base.
Distribution: Local GT + their own website.
Threat level: LOW. Regional only, not a national play yet.
Watch for: If they expand distribution or get funded.

--- General Trends in New Entrants ---
1. Quick commerce is the distribution shortcut — every funded new entrant is on Zepto/Blinkit first
2. Instagram is the marketing first move — aesthetic content + micro-influencer
3. Unique ingredient story is the hook (single-origin, jungle-sourced, craft)
4. D2C website exists but quick commerce is where volumes come from

IMPLICATION FOR NIMBU FRESH: We need a quick commerce optimization strategy. New entrants are using it as a launch pad. We need to own the "established, trusted" positioning vs their novelty play.
